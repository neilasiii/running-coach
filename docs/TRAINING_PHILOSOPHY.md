# Training Philosophy

This document describes the training philosophy that guides all baseline plans in this system.

---

## Core Principles

### 1. Running (VDOT Methodology)
- Progressive volume and intensity through periodized phases
- Time-based workouts, not distance-based
- Quality over quantity with built-in recovery
- Jack Daniels' training zones (E, M, T, I, R)

### 2. Strength Training (Runner-Specific)
- Supports running performance without creating interference
- Scheduled on easy/rest days - never before hard running days
- Focus: core, glutes, hamstrings, calves, stability
- Wrist-free alternatives (forearm planks, goblet holds, pulling movements)

### 3. Mobility & Recovery
- Daily short sessions (10-15 min) targeting runner-specific areas
- Weekly comprehensive sessions (45-60 min)
- Post-run recovery routines (5-10 min)
- Focus areas: hips, hamstrings, calves, thoracic spine, ankles

---

## Integration & Coordination

**Workout Sequencing:**
- Strength on easy/rest days (typically Tue/Fri)
- Quality runs on Thu/Sat with adequate recovery between
- Mobility scaled to training load (lighter on rest days, more comprehensive on recovery days)
- Conservative adjustments if sleep <6 hrs or recovery metrics poor

**Recovery Prioritization:**
- Easy runs kept truly easy (conversational pace, HR-guided)
- Rest days honored, not skipped for makeup workouts
- Quality over quantity - better to skip than compromise with fatigue
- Sleep and recovery metrics guide intensity adjustments

---

## Periodization Framework

Training plans follow this general phase structure:

### Phase 1: Recovery (Post-Race)
- **Duration:** 1-2 weeks
- **Focus:** Physical and mental recovery
- **Running:** Easy pace only, low volume
- **Strength:** None or very light
- **Mobility:** Gentle, recovery-focused

### Phase 2: Base Building
- **Duration:** 3-5 weeks
- **Focus:** Aerobic foundation, volume progression
- **Running:** Primarily easy runs, gradual mileage increase
- **Strength:** Foundation work, 2x/week
- **Mobility:** Pattern building, consistency

### Phase 3: Early Quality
- **Duration:** 3-4 weeks
- **Focus:** Introduction of tempo/threshold work
- **Running:** Easy base + weekly quality session (T pace)
- **Strength:** Strength-endurance emphasis, 2x/week
- **Mobility:** Load management, recovery support

### Phase 4: Race-Specific
- **Duration:** 3-4 weeks (peak training)
- **Focus:** Race-pace work, intervals, specificity
- **Running:** Mix of easy, race-pace, and intervals
- **Strength:** Reduced volume, maintenance
- **Mobility:** Recovery-focused

### Phase 5: Taper
- **Duration:** 1-2 weeks
- **Focus:** Sharpening, freshness
- **Running:** Reduced volume, maintain intensity
- **Strength:** None or very light
- **Mobility:** Minimal maintenance

---

## Adjustment Guidelines

**When to reduce intensity:**
- Sleep <6 hours for 2+ consecutive nights
- Resting HR elevated >5 bpm above baseline
- HRV significantly suppressed
- Lingering muscle soreness or fatigue
- Early signs of illness or injury

**How to adjust:**
1. Reduce intensity first (easy run instead of quality)
2. Reduce duration second (shorten workout by 20-30%)
3. Add rest day if needed (postpone, don't skip completely)
4. Resume normal training when metrics normalize

**Conservative approach:**
- Better to slightly undertrain than overtrain
- Consistency over heroic efforts
- Long-term development over short-term gains
- Sustainable training that integrates with life

## Domain-Specific Philosophies

### Running Philosophy
- Build aerobic base first, quality second
- Pace by effort and HR, not just speed
- Heat/humidity adjustments (slow down as needed)
- Long runs build endurance, quality sessions build speed
- Recovery runs are truly easy (conversational, HR <145)

### Strength Philosophy
- Complements running, doesn't compete with it
- Focus on weaknesses (glutes, hamstrings for most runners)
- Stability and single-leg work over heavy bilateral lifts
- Timing matters (avoid heavy legs before quality running)
- Low DOMS before key workouts

### Mobility Philosophy
- Prevention, not just treatment
- Short daily sessions beat long weekly sessions
- Target runner-specific tightness (calves, hip flexors, hamstrings)
- Post-run mobility aids recovery
- Comprehensive weekly sessions for full-body maintenance

### Nutrition Philosophy
- Fuel for training, recover with nutrition
- Practice race-day fueling during long runs
- Gluten-free, dairy-free compliance (when applicable)
- Hydration with electrolytes, especially in heat
- Energy balance supports training load

---

## Race Preparation

**A-Race Approach:**
- Full periodized training cycle
- Complete taper (1-2 weeks)
- Maximum effort and freshness
- All training builds toward this race

**B-Race Approach:**
- Abbreviated taper (3-7 days easy)
- Strong effort, not maximum
- Used to gauge fitness or as prep race
- Fits within overall training plan

**C-Race Approach:**
- Minimal taper (2-3 days easy)
- Race effort but within training context
- Used for race-pace practice
- Should not compromise key training
