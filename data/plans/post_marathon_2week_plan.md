# Post-Marathon Detailed 2-Week Plan
## Days 1-14 Following November 23, 2025 Marathon

This document provides specific daily guidance for the first 14 days following your marathon. This corresponds to Phase 1 (Immediate Recovery) and Phase 2 (Active Recovery) from the Post-Marathon Recovery Framework.

**Marathon Date:** November 23, 2025 (Saturday)
**Plan Period:** November 24 - December 7, 2025

---

## General Principles for These 14 Days

### Priority Ranking
1. **Rest and recovery** (sleep, nutrition, hydration)
2. **Gentle movement** (walking, light mobility)
3. **Mental restoration** (enjoy the break from structure)
4. **Optional light running** (only if feeling good, never forced)

### Key Guidelines
- **No intensity work whatsoever** (no threshold, no intervals, no tempo, no fast running)
- **No pace targets** (all running is effort-based, conversational only)
- **Stop immediately** if you experience pain, unusual fatigue, or warning signs
- **Flexibility is key** (adjust day-to-day based on how you feel)
- **RHR monitoring** (check morning resting heart rate daily - target: return to 45-47 bpm baseline)

### Coordination with Other Coaches
- **Strength Coach:** Minimal strength work Week 1, light work Week 2
- **Mobility Coach:** Daily light mobility encouraged throughout
- **Nutrition Coach:** Focus on recovery nutrition, anti-inflammatory foods, adequate protein

## Daily Metrics Tracking Recommendations

To guide recovery and progression decisions, track these metrics each morning:

### Essential Daily Metrics
1. **Resting Heart Rate (RHR)**
   - Baseline target: 45-47 bpm
   - Elevated >5 bpm above baseline = extend recovery, reduce intensity
   - Elevated 3-5 bpm = be cautious, consider conservative options

2. **Sleep Quality**
   - Total hours
   - Perceived quality (subjective)
   - Note: Newborn care may affect this - be realistic

3. **Subjective Energy Level**
   - Scale 1-10
   - Note if unusually low

4. **Soreness/Pain**
   - Location and severity
   - Distinguish between normal DOMS and warning sign pain

### Weekly Summary Check
- Total running time
- Number of runs completed
- Any warning signs or concerns
- Overall progression assessment

## Adjustment Protocols for Week 2

### If RHR is Elevated
- **Action:** Choose conservative modification option
- **Example:** Monday calls for 20-30 min easy, do 20 min if RHR is high

### If Sleep Was Poor (<6.5 hours or low quality)
- **Action:** Reduce intensity or skip run if needed
- **Alternative:** Easy walk instead of run

### If Unusual Fatigue or Soreness
- **Action:** Take additional rest day
- **Don't:** Try to "make up" missed runs

### If Pain Appears
- **Action:** Stop running immediately
- **Follow-up:** Address with coaches, extend recovery as needed

### Environmental Adjustments
- **Heat/Humidity:** Slow paces by 15-30 sec/mile, hydrate well, run in morning
- **Cold:** Dress appropriately, allow longer warmup

## Mental Approach for These 14 Days

### Week 1 Mindset
- **Rest is productive** - You are allowing physiological repair
- **No guilt about low activity** - This is necessary for long-term progress
- **Celebrate the marathon achievement** - You earned this break

### Week 2 Mindset
- **Easy means easy** - Don't push paces
- **Consistency over intensity** - Just show up and run gently
- **Patience pays off** - Proper recovery enables next training cycle success

### Overall Philosophy
Recovery is not lost fitness. It is strategic rebuilding. Rushing back leads to injury, burnout, or prolonged fatigue. Trust the process.
