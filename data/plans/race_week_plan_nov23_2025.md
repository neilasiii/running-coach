## Race Strategy

### The First 10K is a Lie
- Your legs will feel AMAZING from the taper
- You will want to run faster
- **DON'T.** Every second too fast in the first 10K costs you 2-3 seconds per mile later
- Target: 10:00-10:15/mi for first 6 miles, even if it feels ridiculously slow

### The Middle Miles (7-18)
- Settle into rhythm: 10:00-10:18/mi
- Stay mechanical: gel every hour, water at every station, salt with gels
- Don't race other runners - run YOUR race
- Assess at mile 13: "How do I feel? Could I do this for another 13?" If no, back off slightly

### The Final 10K (Miles 20-26.2)
- This is where fitness and fueling pay off
- Miles 20-23: **Maintain pace, don't panic if it gets hard** (this is normal)
- Mile 24: Last gel - use as mental boost
- Miles 24-26: **Just hold on** - form over speed
- Last 0.2: **Give what's left**, cross strong

### If Things Go Wrong
- **GI issues:** Slow down, walk if needed, prioritize getting to finish
- **Overheating:** Dump water on head/neck, slow pace, walk through aid stations
- **Bonking/hitting wall:** Take extra gel if available, walk/jog intervals to finish
- **PTT pain:** Assess severity - mild discomfort is okay, sharp pain = consider DNF (your health > one race)
