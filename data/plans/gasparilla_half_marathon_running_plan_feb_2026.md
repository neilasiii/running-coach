## Training Philosophy

This plan follows **Jack Daniels VDOT methodology** with progressive periodization through five distinct phases:

1. **Recovery (Weeks 1-2):** Post-marathon recovery with easy running only
2. **Base Building (Weeks 3-6):** Aerobic foundation with gradual volume increase
3. **Early Quality (Weeks 7-9):** Introduction of tempo work and lactate threshold development
4. **Race-Specific (Weeks 10-11):** Half marathon pace work with peak training load
5. **Taper (Week 12):** Volume reduction while maintaining sharpness

**Key Principles:**
- Time-based workouts (not distance-based)
- Progressive overload with recovery weeks built in
- Quality over quantity - intensity introduced gradually
- Conservative adjustments if sleep/recovery compromised
- Coordination with strength (Tu/Th) and mobility (Sun) schedule

## Workout Details & Modifications

### Easy Runs (E Pace)
**Pace:** 10:04-10:48/mile
**HR:** <145 bpm
**Purpose:** Recovery, aerobic base development, capillary density
**Feel:** Conversational, should be able to speak in full sentences

**Modifications:**
- If sleep <6.5 hrs or RHR elevated >5 bpm: Reduce duration by 10-15 min
- If HR drifting high: Slow to slower end of E pace (10:30-10:48)
- If legs feel heavy: Cut run short, walk remainder

### Strides
**Duration:** 20 seconds each
**Pace:** Relaxed acceleration to ~90% effort (not all-out sprint)
**Recovery:** 90-120 seconds easy jog or walk between
**Purpose:** Neuromuscular recruitment, running economy, leg turnover
**Execution:** Gradual acceleration over first 10 sec, maintain fast-but-controlled for 10 sec, gradual deceleration

**When to skip:** If legs are heavy from previous workout or sleep is compromised

### Tempo Runs (T Pace)
**Pace:** 8:27/mile
**HR:** 160-170 bpm
**Purpose:** Improve lactate clearance, raise sustainable threshold pace
**Feel:** "Comfortably hard" - could speak a few words but not full sentences

**Modifications:**
- Conservative: Reduce reps or duration by 20% (e.g., 2x10min → 2x8min)
- Moderate: Reduce pace by 5-10 sec/mile (e.g., 8:35-8:40 instead of 8:27)
- If HR >175 during tempo: Slow immediately to bring HR down to 165-170

**Execution Tips:**
- Start first rep slightly slower (8:35) and settle into 8:27
- Focus on smooth, controlled breathing
- Recovery jogs should bring HR down to 130-140 before next rep

### Interval Runs (I Pace)
**Pace:** 7:55/mile
**HR:** 170-180 bpm
**Purpose:** VO2 max development, improve aerobic power
**Feel:** Hard effort, challenging to speak

**Modifications:**
- If recovering poorly: Skip and replace with easy run
- Conservative: Reduce number of reps (5x4min → 4x4min)
- If HR >185: Ease off slightly to keep HR in 170-180 range

**Execution Tips:**
- Intervals should feel hard but sustainable across all reps
- Last rep should be same effort as first (not faster)
- Focus on form - don't let shoulders tense up

### Half Marathon Pace Runs
**Pace:** 8:33/mile (B-goal pace)
**HR:** 160-165 bpm
**Purpose:** Race-specific preparation, mental confidence, pacing practice
**Feel:** Moderate-hard, could say a few words with effort

**Modifications:**
- If not feeling good: Run at 8:42-8:45 (C-goal pace) instead
- If HR drifting >170: You're going too fast, ease back to 8:40-8:45

**Execution Tips:**
- These are dress rehearsals for race day
- Practice race-day breathing patterns
- Practice positive self-talk during effort
- Stay relaxed, especially shoulders and hands

## Recovery Guidelines

### When to Modify or Skip Workouts

**Reduce Intensity (run easy instead of quality):**
- Sleep <6.5 hours
- Resting HR elevated 3-5 bpm above baseline
- Sleep quality score <50/100
- Mild muscle soreness
- Elevated stress or low body battery

**Reduce Duration (cut workout by 20-30%):**
- Sleep 6.5-7 hours but quality poor
- Resting HR elevated 2-3 bpm
- Minor lingering fatigue from previous workout
- Scheduling conflicts requiring shorter window

**Skip Workout Entirely:**
- Sleep <6 hours
- Resting HR elevated >5 bpm above baseline
- Illness or injury symptoms
- Excessive muscle soreness preventing good form
- Multiple nights of poor sleep in a row

**General Rule:** It's always better to undertrain by 10% than overtrain by 5%. Missing one workout is not a problem. Pushing through compromised recovery can derail weeks of training.

- Calves and ankles
- Hamstrings
- Thoracic spine

**Post-Workout Mobility:**
- 5-10 min after every run
- Focus on calves, hip flexors, quads
- Especially important after tempo and interval sessions

### Posterior Tibialis Tendinopathy Management
**Prevention:**
- Adequate warmup before all runs
- Post-run calf stretching and ankle mobility
- Strengthen tibialis anterior and peroneals
- Monitor for early warning signs (tightness, mild pain)
- Reduce volume immediately if symptoms appear
