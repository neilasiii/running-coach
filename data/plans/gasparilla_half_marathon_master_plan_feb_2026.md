## Training Philosophy

This plan integrates **three coaching domains** working in harmony:

1. **Running (VDOT Running Coach):** Jack Daniels methodology, time-based workouts, progressive volume and intensity
2. **Strength (Runner Strength Coach):** Full-body workouts respecting wrist constraint, coordinated timing to avoid interference
3. **Mobility (Mobility Coach):** Daily PTT management, Sunday comprehensive sessions, post-run recovery protocols

**Key Principle:** Each domain supports the others. Strength builds resilience, mobility prevents injury, running drives adaptation.

## Recovery & Modification Guidelines

### Reduce Training Load If:

**Sleep:**
- <6 hours for 2+ consecutive nights → Reduce intensity 50%, convert quality to easy
- <5 hours → REST day

**Resting Heart Rate:**
- +3-5 bpm above baseline → Monitor closely, reduce intensity if persists
- +5+ bpm → REST or very easy walk only

**Subjective Feel:**
- Persistent fatigue → Add rest day
- Acute soreness/pain → Modify or skip
- Illness → REST until recovered

### Modification Hierarchy:

1. **Reduce intensity** (convert quality to easy pace)
2. **Reduce volume** (cut workout duration 25-50%)
3. **Add rest day** (skip planned workout)
4. **Extend recovery** (take 2-3 days off if severely fatigued)

**Philosophy:** Better to undertrain 10% than overtrain 5%. One missed workout won't derail the plan; overtraining will.

## Key Success Factors

1. **Consistency over intensity** - Show up for every planned session
2. **Recovery is training** - Sleep, mobility, nutrition are non-negotiable
3. **Listen to your body** - Modify when needed, don't push through warning signs
4. **PTT management** - Daily foot/ankle work prevents flare-ups
5. **Coordination across domains** - Running, strength, mobility work together
6. **Progressive overload** - Small improvements each week compound over 12 weeks
7. **Taper trust** - Less is more in race week, arrive fresh not fatigued
