# Post-Marathon 2-Week Mobility Plan
## Detailed Daily Implementation for Marathon Recovery

**Race Date:** November 23, 2025 (Central Florida Legends Skunk Ape Marathon)
**Target Finish:** Sub-4:30 (B Goal), aspiring for Sub-4:00 (A Goal)
**Plan Duration:** Days 1-14 post-marathon
**Created:** November 13, 2025

**Companion Document:** `data/frameworks/post_marathon_mobility_framework.md`

---

## HOW TO USE THIS PLAN

### Daily Structure

Each day provides:
- **Morning routine** (when applicable, considering work schedule Mon-Thu)
- **Evening routine** (primary mobility session on workdays)
- **Optional additions** (for good recovery days or weekend time)
- **Coordination notes** (running, strength, sleep considerations)

### Three Duration Options

Every session offers:
- **SHORT (5-10 min):** Minimum effective dose, for low-energy days
- **MEDIUM (10-20 min):** Standard recommendation, most days
- **LONG (20-30+ min):** For weekends or when time/energy allows

**Recommendation:** Default to MEDIUM on weekdays, LONG on weekends.

### Adjustment Factors

**If Sleep Was Poor (<6 hrs):**
- Default to SHORT versions
- Increase session frequency instead of duration
- Focus on restorative positions, breathing

**If Feeling Better Than Expected:**
- Can use LONG versions earlier
- Don't skip phases entirely - tissue healing has a timeline

**If Feeling Worse Than Expected:**
- Extend Phase 1 an extra day or two
- Medical evaluation if pain is sharp/localized/asymmetrical

## PHASE 1: ACUTE RECOVERY (Days 1-3)

### DAY 1 - RACE DAY (November 23, 2025)

**MORNING:**
Pre-race mobility: None needed (taper has prepared you)

**IMMEDIATELY POST-RACE (Within 30 min):**
- Walk 5-10 min (don't sit/lie down immediately)
- Drink 16-24 oz water + electrolytes
- Change into compression socks if available

**AFTERNOON (2-4 hours post-race):**

**SHORT (5 min):**
1. Supine knee hugs - 10 gentle reps per leg
2. Ankle circles - 10 each direction, both ankles
3. Diaphragmatic breathing - 3 minutes lying down

**MEDIUM (10 min):**
1. Supine knee hugs - 10 reps per leg
2. Supine hip circles - 5 circles each direction, each leg
3. Ankle pumps and circles - 15 reps
4. Gentle spinal twist - 30 sec per side (lying)
5. Diaphragmatic breathing - 5 minutes

**LONG (15 min):**
Add to MEDIUM:
- Cat/cow (very gentle) - 8 reps
- Child's pose (supported) - 2 minutes
- Legs up wall - 5 minutes (promotes venous return)

**EVENING:**

**SHORT (5 min):**
- Legs up wall - 5 minutes (best recovery tool)
- Breathing focus - relaxation emphasis

**MEDIUM (10 min):**
1. Easy walk around house/block - 5 min
2. Supine knee hugs - 8 reps per leg
3. Ankle circles - 10 each direction
4. Legs up wall - 5 minutes
5. Breathing - 3 minutes

**LONG (20 min):**
1. Gentle walk (flat, easy pace) - 10 min
2. Supine sequence:
   - Knee hugs - 10 reps per leg
   - Hip circles - 5 each direction, each leg
   - Spinal twist - 1 min per side
3. Legs up wall - 5-7 minutes
4. Diaphragmatic breathing - 5 minutes

**COORDINATION NOTES:**
- Running: Complete rest (walking only)
- Strength: None
- Nutrition: Rehydrate, protein + carbs within 90 min of finish
- Sleep: Elevate legs, compression socks overnight if comfortable
- **PTT Monitoring:** Ice medial ankle if any specific irritation noted

**RED FLAGS:** Sharp pain, significant swelling, dark urine, asymmetrical pain → medical evaluation

## ADAPTIVE GUIDANCE

### If Recovery is Slower Than Expected

**Extend each phase by 2-3 days:**
- Phase 1 (Days 1-3) → Days 1-5
- Phase 2 (Days 4-7) → Days 6-10
- Phase 3 (Days 8-14) → Days 11-17

**Indicators for extended recovery:**
- Significant soreness persisting beyond Day 5
- Pain (not soreness) in specific areas
- Asymmetrical symptoms
- Fatigue that doesn't improve with rest
- Sleep continues to be severely disrupted

### If Recovery is Faster Than Expected

**Can compress slightly, but don't skip phases:**
- Advance to LONG versions earlier
- Add light strength by Day 6-7
- Resume quality running by Day 12-13

**Don't rush:** Tissue healing happens on a biological timeline. Feeling good doesn't mean tissues are 100% healed.

### If Injury Emerges

**Stop and evaluate:**
- Sharp pain → medical evaluation
- Asymmetrical symptoms → medical evaluation
- Pain that worsens with activity → rest and evaluate
- Localized swelling → ice, elevate, evaluate

**PTT Specific:**
- Medial ankle pain → return to PT protocol immediately
- Ice, rest, reassess running progression
- May need extended recovery before full training
