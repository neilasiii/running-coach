# 14-Day Post-Marathon Mobility Plan
## Nov 28 - Dec 11, 2025

**Athlete:** Neil Stagner
**Context:** 5 days post-marathon (Nov 23, 2025)
**Recovery Status:** Training readiness 1-25/100 (critically low), sleep improving
**Plan Focus:** Gentle recovery mobility → Progressive reactivation → Return to maintenance

**Key Principles:**
- Week 1 (Days 1-7): Gentle, restorative work - promote circulation without creating fatigue
- Week 2 (Days 8-14): Progressive reactivation as running returns - support tissue remodeling
- Avoid aggressive stretching/tissue work that creates soreness
- Prioritize sleep and passive recovery - mobility supports but doesn't replace rest

---

## WEEK 1: Gentle Recovery Phase (Nov 28 - Dec 4)
**Training Context:** Minimal/no running, focus on rest and gentle movement

### Day 1 - Friday, Nov 28
**Session:** Gentle Lower Body Circulation (10-15 min)
**Timing:** Evening preferred

**Routine:**
1. Supine breathing: 2 min (belly breathing, relaxation)
2. Gentle knee circles: 10/direction each leg
3. Figure-4 hip rocks: 30 sec/side (light stretch, no forcing)
4. Supine hamstring sweep: 8/side (bent knee to straight, gentle)
5. Calf stretch against wall: 45 sec/side (gentle)
6. Ankle circles: 10/direction each foot
7. Cat-cow: 10 slow reps (spinal mobility)

**Rationale:** Light movement promotes blood flow to recovering tissues without creating additional stress. Focus on gentle range of motion, not deep stretching.

---

### Day 2 - Saturday, Nov 29
**Session:** Extended Restorative Mobility (20-30 min)
**Timing:** Mid-morning or afternoon

**Routine:**
1. Diaphragmatic breathing (supine): 3 min
2. Supine IT band foam roll: 60 sec/side (gentle pressure)
3. Quad foam roll: 60 sec/side (avoid aggressive pressure)
4. Glute foam roll: 60 sec/side
5. 90/90 hip stretch: 90 sec/side (seated position)
6. Pigeon pose (supported): 90 sec/side (use yoga block for support)
7. Prone hip flexor stretch: 60 sec/side (couch stretch variation, gentle)
8. Supine spinal twist: 60 sec/side
9. Child's pose: 2 min (parasympathetic activation)
10. Legs-up-wall: 3-5 min (optional, promotes venous return)

**Rationale:** Longer session on weekend with more time. Foam rolling uses gentle pressure for tissue hydration. Static stretches held longer but without forcing - you're not chasing range, just maintaining mobility during recovery.

---

### Day 3 - Sunday, Nov 30
**Session:** Gentle Yoga/Mobility Flow (20-25 min)
**Timing:** Morning or evening

**Routine:**
1. Cat-cow flow: 10 reps
2. Downward dog: 60 sec (pedal feet gently)
3. Low lunge (static): 60 sec/side
4. Pyramid pose (modified): 45 sec/side
5. Garland pose (deep squat): 60 sec
6. Seated forward fold: 90 sec (gentle, knees bent if needed)
7. Supine figure-4: 90 sec/side
8. Reclined hamstring stretch (strap): 60 sec/side
9. Spinal twist: 60 sec/side
10. Savasana: 3-5 min (full relaxation)

**Rationale:** Sunday yoga session addresses full-body mobility with emphasis on hips, hamstrings, calves - primary areas stressed during marathon. Flow-based approach keeps tissues warm.

## DURATION OPTIONS FOR EACH SESSION

Every routine above can be modified:

**SHORT (5-8 min):** Select 5-6 key exercises, reduce hold times to 30-45 sec
**MEDIUM (10-15 min):** As prescribed above
**LONG (20-30 min):** Add 2-3 additional exercises, extend hold times to 90-120 sec, include legs-up-wall or extended relaxation

---

## EQUIPMENT ALTERNATIVES

**No foam roller:** Use lacrosse ball for targeted work, extend static stretching time
**No resistance bands:** Focus on bodyweight movements, extend hold times
**No yoga blocks:** Use couch/chair for support in pigeon pose and similar positions
**Minimal equipment:** All routines can be completed with bodyweight only

---

## INTEGRATION NOTES

**Coordination with Running:**
- Week 1: Mobility primary recovery tool (minimal running)
- Week 2: Mobility supports return to easy running
- Dynamic mobility before runs, static mobility 3+ hours after runs or evening

**Coordination with Strength:**
- Post-strength mobility focuses on tissue recovery, not deep stretching
- Save intensive mobility for non-strength days
- Tuesday/Friday light mobility complements strength schedule

**Sleep Priority:**
- If sleep <6.5 hours, reduce session duration by 30-50%
- Focus on parasympathetic work (breathing, gentle stretches, relaxation)
- Evening sessions should promote relaxation, not create additional stress

---

## PROGRESSION NOTES

**Week 1 Focus:** Rest, gentle movement, circulation
**Week 2 Focus:** Reactivation, supporting return to running, tissue remodeling

**After Day 14:**
- Resume normal mobility cadence: 5-10 min weekdays, 20-30 min Sunday
- Dynamic mobility before quality sessions
- Maintenance work on easy days
- Intensive mobility on rest days or separate from hard efforts
