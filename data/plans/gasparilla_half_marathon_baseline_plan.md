## Training Philosophy & Integration

This plan integrates three coaching domains:

**1. Running (VDOT Methodology):**
- Progressive volume and intensity through 5 phases
- Time-based workouts, not distance-based
- Quality over quantity with built-in recovery

**2. Strength Training (Runner-Specific):**
- Full-body sessions 2x/week (Tuesday primary, Friday lighter)
- NO wrist pressure - all alternatives use forearms, goblet holds, or pulling movements
- Supports running without creating interference

**3. Mobility & Recovery:**
- Daily PTT management (10-15 min)
- Sunday comprehensive sessions (45-60 min)
- Post-run recovery routines (5-10 min)

**Coordination Principles:**
- Strength on easy/rest days (Tue/Fri) - never before hard running days
- Quality runs on Thu/Sat with adequate recovery between
- Mobility every day, scaled to training load
- Conservative adjustments if sleep <6 hrs or recovery metrics poor

## PTT (Posterior Tibialis Tendinopathy) Daily Protocol

**Essential Exercises (10-15 min daily):**

1. **Towel Scrunches:** 3 × 15 reps per foot
2. **Single-Leg Eccentric Heel Raises:** 3 × 8-10 per leg (3-5 sec lower)
3. **Toe Yoga:** 2 × 10 reps per foot
4. **Banded Ankle Inversions:** 3 × 15 reps per foot
5. **Standing Arch Doming:** 6 × 10 sec holds per foot

**Frequency:** Daily, every day of the plan
**Timing:** Evening preferred, or post-run on run days
**Purpose:** Prevent PTT flare-ups, maintain foot/ankle health

## Adjustment Guidelines

**If Sleep <6 hours or quality <50:**
- Convert quality runs to easy runs
- Reduce long run by 10-15 min
- Keep easy pace at upper end of range (10:30-10:48/mile)
- Add 5-10 min to warmup

**If Resting HR elevated >5 bpm above baseline (45 bpm):**
- Take extra rest day
- Convert next run to easy effort
- Monitor for illness/overtraining

**If PTT symptoms appear (medial ankle pain):**
- Reduce running volume by 30%
- Increase PTT drills to 2x daily
- Avoid hills and speed work until resolved
- Consider shoes with more arch support

**If strength soreness interferes with running:**
- Reduce strength volume by 25%
- Focus on single-leg work with lighter loads
- Ensure 48 hrs between strength and quality runs

## Key Success Factors

1. **Consistency over intensity** - showing up for easy runs matters more than crushing workouts
2. **Respect the taper** - trust the training, resist urge to add volume in final weeks
3. **Sleep prioritization** - 7+ hrs/night, especially before quality sessions
4. **PTT daily attention** - 10-15 min every day prevents issues
5. **Pace discipline** - easy runs EASY (10:04-10:48), tempo at prescribed T pace
6. **Strength supports running** - never sacrifice running quality for strength gains
7. **Listen to your body** - adjust when needed, conservative approach prevents injury
