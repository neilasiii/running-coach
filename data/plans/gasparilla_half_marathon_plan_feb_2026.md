## Race Week Strategy

### Race Day Protocol

**Pre-Race (2-3 hours before):**
- Light breakfast: familiar gluten-free, dairy-free option (oatmeal with banana, rice cakes with almond butter)
- Hydration: 16-20 oz water with electrolytes
- Arrive at race site 60-90 min early for parking, bib pickup, logistics

**Warmup (20-30 min before start):**
- 10-15 min very easy jogging
- 4-6 strides building to race pace
- Final bathroom stop
- Line up in appropriate corral (sub-1:50 or sub-2:00 based on goal)

**Race Pacing Strategy:**

**Miles 1-3 (Controlled Start):**
- Target: 8:30-8:35 pace (slightly conservative)
- Purpose: Avoid early adrenaline surge, establish rhythm
- Keep effort relaxed, breathing controlled
- Focus on efficient form

**Miles 4-7 (Settle In):**
- Target: 8:24-8:30 pace (A goal range) OR 8:30-8:35 (B goal range)
- Purpose: Lock into sustainable race pace
- Monitor HR and breathing — should feel "comfortably hard"
- Stay relaxed through shoulders and jaw

**Miles 8-10 (Assessment Point):**
- Target: Maintain current pace
- Purpose: Assess how effort feels before final push
- If feeling strong: maintain or slightly increase effort
- If struggling: adjust to B or C goal pace, avoid blowing up

**Miles 11-13.1 (Finish Strong):**
- Target: Hold pace or slight increase if feeling good
- Purpose: Empty the tank intelligently
- Miles 11-12: maintain rhythm, focus on form
- Mile 13-finish: push hard, use crowd energy, finish strong

**Goal Execution Hierarchy:**
1. **A Goal (Sub-1:50):** Requires 8:24/mile average — only pursue if feeling strong at mile 8
2. **B Goal (Sub-1:52):** Requires 8:33/mile average — primary target, conservative start
3. **C Goal (Sub-1:54):** Requires 8:42/mile average — fallback if conditions/fatigue demand

**Fueling Plan:**
- **Pre-race:** Gel 15 min before start (optional, based on preference)
- **During race:** Water at miles 3, 6, 9, 11 (aid stations)
- **Electrolytes:** Consider electrolyte drink at miles 6 and 9 if warm/humid
- **Gel:** Mile 6-7 if feeling energy dip (optional for half marathon distance)

## Environmental & Scheduling Adjustments

**Heat/Humidity Adjustments (Central Florida):**
- Easy pace: Add 15-30 sec/mile in heat (>75°F, >60% humidity)
- Threshold/Interval pace: Effort-based vs. strict pace adherence
- Hydration: Carry water for runs >60 min in warm conditions
- Timing: Early morning sessions strongly preferred in summer-like conditions

**Work Schedule Integration:**
- Monday/Wednesday easy runs: Early morning (0700-0730 start) or evening if needed
- Thursday quality sessions: Use work fitness time (0715 start ideal)
- Weekend long runs: Saturday morning preferred, allows full recovery before Monday

**Sleep & Recovery Monitoring:**
- Track resting HR: Elevated RHR >5 bpm above baseline → easy day or rest
- Sleep <6.5 hours → consider conservative workout adjustment
- Persistent fatigue → swap quality session for easy run, shift schedule

**Newborn Care Adjustments:**
- Plan flexibility built into weekly structure
- If quality session must shift, prioritize getting it done vs. specific day
- Easy runs can be broken into 2 shorter sessions if needed
- Recovery always takes priority over hitting exact prescribed volume

## Modification Options

**If Recovery Compromised (poor sleep, elevated RHR, fatigue):**

**Threshold Workouts:**
- Conservative: Reduce to 2 reps instead of 3, maintain time per rep
- Moderate: Reduce time per rep by 20% (e.g., 10 min → 8 min), maintain rep count
- Skip: Convert to 50 min E if truly compromised

**Interval Workouts:**
- Conservative: Reduce rep count by 1-2, maintain intensity
- Moderate: Maintain reps, reduce time per rep
- Skip: Convert to 45 min E with 4-6 strides

**Long Runs:**
- Conservative: Reduce by 15-20 min, maintain easy effort
- Moderate: Complete duration, skip any planned quality segments
- Skip: Replace with 60 min E if severely compromised

**General Principles:**
- Maintain easy run consistency (these are most important)
- Quality sessions can shift within the week
- Don't skip 2+ quality sessions in a row (indicates need for recovery week)
- Never "make up" missed sessions by stacking intensity
