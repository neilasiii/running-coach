## Quick Decision Trees

### "Should I do strength today?"

**Week 1 (Days 1-7):** NO. Always no. Complete rest.

**Week 2 (Days 8-14):** Only on designated days (10 and 13), and only if:
- [ ] No residual marathon soreness
- [ ] RHR at baseline (45-47 bpm)
- [ ] Slept 5+ hours
- [ ] Feel energized

**Week 3+:** Follow framework principles:
- [ ] Tuesday and Friday are preferred days
- [ ] Not within 24 hours of quality running
- [ ] RHR within 3 bpm of baseline
- [ ] Sleep adequate (6+ hours)
- [ ] No excessive running fatigue

**If any checklist items fail:** Execute Conservative Option or skip session

---

### "Which strength session should I do?"

**Week 2, Day 10 (First session):**
- Use Session 1 from 2-week plan (page for Day 10)
- Conservative Option if recovery questionable

**Week 2, Day 13 (Second session):**
- Use Session 2 from 2-week plan (page for Day 13)
- Full Option if Day 10 caused zero soreness
- Conservative Option if Day 10 caused any soreness

**Week 3+:**
- Reference framework document, Phase 3 section
- Build Tuesday (primary) and Friday (supporting) sessions
- Use movement pattern library for exercise selection

---

### "How do I know if I'm progressing appropriately?"

**Week 2 Success Markers:**
- Zero soreness from either strength session
- RHR remained at baseline throughout week
- Sessions felt easy (RPE 4-5)
- Movements controlled and confident
- Energized after sessions, not depleted

**Week 3-6 Success Markers:**
- Consistent 2 sessions/week
- Progressive loading (5-10% increases when appropriate)
- No strength-related injuries
- Zero PTT flares attributable to strength work
- Strength sessions don't compromise running

**Week 7-12 Success Markers:**
- Loading at RPE 7-8 for primary movements
- Visible strength improvements (more weight, better control)
- Enhanced running economy (less effort at same paces)
- Durable during long runs
- Confident in movement patterns

## Critical Reminders

### Post-Marathon Recovery is NOT Linear

Your recovery will fluctuate based on:
- Sleep quality (newborn care creates variability)
- Running volume increases
- Life stress
- Nutrition adequacy

**Response:** Adjust strength training daily based on recovery markers, not a rigid schedule.

---

### Strength Training Serves Running

During post-marathon recovery, strength training becomes a higher priority than during race-specific training. BUT:

- Strength never compromises running workouts
- If in doubt, skip or reduce strength
- Quality running sessions take precedence once they begin (Week 7+)
- The goal is to enter the next training cycle STRONGER, not broken

---

### The Zero-Soreness Goal (Week 2)

Week 2 strength sessions should create ZERO next-day soreness. This seems counterintuitive but is essential:

**Why zero soreness?**
- Proves movement patterns are ready for loading
- Demonstrates appropriate volume for recovery state
- Builds confidence without creating fatigue
- Establishes baseline for progressive overload

**If you're sore:**
- You did too much
- Reduce volume for next session
- Extend Week 2 structure before progressing to Week 3

---

### PTT Management is Non-Negotiable

Given your history of posterior tibialis tendinopathy:

**Green Light Signs:**
- No medial ankle discomfort during sessions
- No tenderness post-session
- Normal walking gait

**Yellow Light Signs:**
- Mild discomfort during single-leg work
- Slight stiffness post-session
- **Action:** Reduce single-leg loading 50%, avoid eccentric calf work

**Red Light Signs:**
- Pain during exercises
- Compensatory movement patterns
- Next-day limping
- **Action:** STOP strength work, return to PT protocol

## Troubleshooting Quick Reference

**Problem:** "I'm still sore on Day 8"
- **Solution:** Extend Phase 1 (complete rest) through Day 9-10. Don't start strength until soreness resolves.

**Problem:** "Week 2 strength session made me sore"
- **Solution:** Use Conservative Option for next session. Repeat Week 2 before progressing to Week 3.

**Problem:** "My PTT is flaring up"
- **Solution:** Stop strength immediately. Return to PT protocol. Reference framework document PTT section.

**Problem:** "I have terrible sleep (newborn)"
- **Solution:** <5 hrs = skip strength. 5-6.5 hrs = Conservative Option only. >6.5 hrs = Full Option.

**Problem:** "Week 2 feels too easy"
- **Solution:** Good! That's the design. Don't rush. If genuinely easy, you're ready for Week 3 progression.

**Problem:** "Can I add a third strength session?"
- **Solution:** No. Two quality sessions beat three mediocre sessions. Focus on recovery and running.
