# Post-Marathon Recovery Framework

This document provides an overarching framework for post-marathon recovery and progression back to training. This is a reference guide to be consulted throughout the recovery period and into the next training cycle.

**Last Updated:** November 13, 2025

---

## Recovery Philosophy

Marathon recovery is not simply "time off" but a structured progression from complete rest to active recovery to base rebuilding. The goal is to allow physiological repair while maintaining fitness, then systematically return to structured training.

**Key Principle:** Recovery is not lost fitness. It is strategic rebuilding that prevents injury and enables long-term progression.

---

## Recovery Timeline Overview

### Phase 1: Immediate Recovery (Days 1-7)
**Duration:** 7 days
**Focus:** Rest, repair, light movement only
**Running:** Minimal to none

### Phase 2: Active Recovery (Days 8-14)
**Duration:** 7 days
**Focus:** Easy aerobic activity, gradual return to movement
**Running:** Short easy runs, optional cross-training

### Phase 3: Base Rebuilding (Days 15-28)
**Duration:** 14 days
**Focus:** Gradual volume increase, aerobic consistency
**Running:** Consistent easy running, building weekly volume

### Phase 4: Return to Structure (Days 29+)
**Duration:** Ongoing
**Focus:** Reintroduce quality work, begin next training cycle
**Running:** Structured workouts return

---

## Phase 1: Immediate Recovery (Days 1-7)

### Purpose
Allow major physiological systems to repair:
- Muscle fiber damage and inflammation
- Glycogen depletion
- Immune system suppression
- Nervous system fatigue
- Connective tissue stress

### General Guidelines
- **Rest is primary activity**
- **Light walking is encouraged** (10-30 min daily)
- **No running** (or very minimal if needed for mental health)
- **Sleep prioritization** (aim for 8+ hours)
- **Nutrition focus:** Protein, carbohydrates, anti-inflammatory foods
- **Hydration:** Return to normal hydration status

### Running Prescriptions (if any)
- **Days 1-3:** Complete rest from running strongly recommended
- **Days 4-7:** Optional 15-20 min very easy shuffles IF legs feel good and you feel restless
- **Effort:** Zone 1 only, conversational, no pace goals
- **Stop immediately if:** Pain, excessive soreness, or unusual fatigue

### Strength Training
- **Days 1-3:** Complete rest
- **Days 4-7:** Optional light bodyweight mobility work only (no resistance)
- **Avoid:** Any lower-body resistance, plyometrics, or eccentric loading

### Mobility Work
- **Daily:** Light stretching, foam rolling (gentle), yoga flows
- **Focus areas:** Calves, quads, hips, IT band, feet
- **Avoid:** Aggressive deep tissue work in first 3-4 days

### Readiness Indicators to Progress
- Normal walking gait (no limping or stiffness)
- Resting heart rate returns to baseline (+/- 2-3 bpm)
- Sleep quality normalized
- No lingering muscle soreness beyond mild DOMS
- Mental readiness to resume light activity

---

## Phase 2: Active Recovery (Days 8-14)

### Purpose
Reintroduce light aerobic stress to:
- Restore muscular coordination
- Reactivate aerobic pathways
- Rebuild movement confidence
- Begin psychological transition back to training

### General Guidelines
- **Easy running returns** (short durations, low frequency)
- **Continue prioritizing recovery** (sleep, nutrition, hydration)
- **No intensity work whatsoever**
- **Optional cross-training** (swimming, cycling, walking)

### Running Prescriptions
- **Frequency:** 3-4 runs maximum
- **Duration:** 20-40 min per run
- **Effort:** Easy (E) only, truly conversational
- **Pace:** No pace targets; run by feel, 10:30-11:30/mi range likely
- **Pattern:** Every other day running at most
- **Example Week:** Mon rest, Tue 20 min E, Wed rest, Thu 25 min E, Fri rest, Sat 30 min E, Sun rest/walk

### Strength Training
- **Resume light strength:** 2 sessions, bodyweight or very light resistance
- **Focus:** Core, single-leg balance, glute activation
- **Avoid:** Heavy loading, significant eccentric work, high-rep fatigue sets

### Mobility Work
- **Continue daily light work:** 10-15 min per day
- **Sunday longer session:** 30-45 min
- **Begin deeper work if tissues feel ready**

### Readiness Indicators to Progress
- Running feels fluid and natural, not labored
- No residual soreness between runs
- Resting HR stable at baseline
- Sleep consistent and restorative
- Appetite and energy levels normal
- No pain or lingering injury concerns

---

## Phase 3: Base Rebuilding (Days 15-28)

### Purpose
Gradually increase aerobic training load:
- Build weekly volume back toward pre-taper levels
- Re-establish running consistency
- Strengthen connective tissues for future intensity
- Prepare body for structured training

### General Guidelines
- **Increase frequency and duration gradually**
- **Stay in easy (E) zone exclusively**
- **No threshold, interval, or marathon-pace work yet**
- **Optional strides** (4-6 × 20 sec) toward end of phase for neuromuscular activation

### Running Prescriptions
- **Frequency:** 4 runs per week
- **Weekly Volume Progression:**
  - Week 1 (Days 15-21): ~2.5-3 hours total
  - Week 2 (Days 22-28): ~3-3.5 hours total
- **Effort:** Easy (E) pace, 10:00-11:10/mi range when fresh
- **Long Run:** Return to 60-75 min long run by end of phase
- **Pattern:** Mon E, Wed E, Thu E, Sat Long
- **Optional strides:** Add 4-6 × 20 sec strides to 1-2 runs in Week 2

### Strength Training
- **Resume normal schedule:** 2 sessions per week (Tue primary, Fri lighter)
- **Intensity:** Moderate resistance, focus on form and activation
- **Exercises:** Full runner strength program (glutes, core, single-leg, calves)
- **Coordination:** Avoid heavy lower-body work within 24 hours of runs

### Mobility Work
- **Return to normal structure:** Daily brief work + Sunday longer session
- **Focus:** Maintain range of motion, address any lingering tightness

### Readiness Indicators to Progress
- Consistently completing easy runs feeling fresh
- Weekly volume tolerance back to ~4 hours
- Running feels smooth and unrestricted
- No warning signs of overuse injury
- RHR stable and consistent
- Sleep and recovery metrics solid
- Mental readiness for structured work

---

## Phase 4: Return to Structure (Days 29+)

### Purpose
Transition from base rebuilding to structured training:
- Begin next training cycle (e.g., Gasparilla Half Marathon prep)
- Reintroduce quality work systematically
- Reassess VDOT based on fresh workout performance
- Establish new training cycle periodization

### General Guidelines
- **Enter Phase 1 of new training cycle** (Base/Foundation)
- **Gradual reintroduction of quality work**
- **VDOT reassessment needed** before prescribing T/I paces
- **Coordinate with other coaches** for full training integration

### Running Prescriptions
- **Week 1 (Days 29-35):** Continue easy running, add 4-6 strides 2x/week
- **Week 2 (Days 36-42):** Optional VDOT assessment workout (e.g., 2 × 1 mile T effort with full recovery)
- **Week 3+:** Begin Phase 1 of next training block (see new training plan)

### VDOT Reassessment Protocol
Before beginning structured training, assess current fitness:

**Option 1: Threshold Assessment**
- Warmup: 15 min E + 4 strides
- Main: 2 × 1 mile at T effort (comfortably hard) with 3 min jog recovery
- Cooldown: 10 min E
- Use average pace to estimate VDOT

**Option 2: Time Trial**
- Warmup: 15 min E + 6 strides
- Main: 5K time trial at hard, controlled effort
- Cooldown: 10 min E
- Use 5K time for VDOT calculation

**Recommended timing:** Days 36-42 (5-6 weeks post-marathon)

### Strength and Mobility
- **Resume full normal schedule:** Integrated with running plan
- **Strength:** 2 sessions/week, full intensity as tolerated
- **Mobility:** Daily brief + Sunday longer session

## Key Recovery Milestones and Checkpoints

### Week 1 Post-Marathon
- Walking normally without stiffness
- RHR returning to baseline
- Sleep improving

### Week 2 Post-Marathon
- Able to run 20-30 min easily without residual soreness
- No pain or injury concerns
- Energy levels improving

### Week 3 Post-Marathon
- Running 4x/week feels sustainable
- Volume back to 2.5-3 hours/week
- Ready for light strength work

### Week 4 Post-Marathon
- Volume approaching pre-taper levels (3.5-4 hours/week)
- Considering VDOT reassessment
- Feeling mentally ready for quality work

### Week 5-6 Post-Marathon
- VDOT reassessment complete
- Ready to begin structured training
- All systems (strength, mobility, running) coordinated

## Warning Signs: When to Extend Recovery

Do not progress to the next phase if you experience:

### Physical Warning Signs
- Persistent muscle soreness beyond expected DOMS
- Joint pain (knees, ankles, hips)
- Unusual fatigue during easy runs
- RHR elevated >5 bpm above baseline for multiple days
- Poor sleep quality or insomnia
- Recurrence of old injury symptoms

### Performance Warning Signs
- Easy pace feels harder than expected at same HR
- Unable to maintain conversational effort
- Significantly slower paces at equivalent perceived effort
- Declining performance across multiple runs

### Psychological Warning Signs
- Lack of motivation to run
- Running feels like a chore, not enjoyable
- Dreading workouts
- Mental fatigue or burnout signs

**Action if warning signs appear:**
- Add 3-7 days of easier activity
- Reduce running frequency or duration
- Consult with coaches about extended recovery
- Address root causes (sleep, stress, nutrition, overtraining)

## Environmental and Contextual Adjustments

### Sleep-Deprived Recovery (Newborn Care)
If sleep quality remains compromised:
- **Extend each phase by 3-5 days**
- **Reduce running frequency** (3 runs/week instead of 4)
- **Prioritize rest days over consistency**
- **Use RHR as primary recovery indicator**
- **Be conservative with progression**

### Hot/Humid Conditions
When temperatures are warm during recovery:
- **Run in coolest part of day** (early morning)
- **Slow paces by 15-30 sec/mile**
- **Hydrate aggressively**
- **Shorten run durations if heat stress is high**

### Work Schedule Constraints
Given Mon-Thu work schedule (0700-1730):
- **Prioritize morning runs** when possible
- **Use Friday/Saturday/Sunday** for longer or key sessions
- **Don't force runs when schedule doesn't allow**
- **Flexibility over rigid structure during recovery**

## Nutrition and Fueling During Recovery

### Week 1: Immediate Recovery
- **Carbohydrates:** Return to normal intake (no need for aggressive carb loading)
- **Protein:** 20-30g per meal for muscle repair
- **Anti-inflammatory foods:** Omega-3s, berries, leafy greens, turmeric
- **Hydration:** Restore fluid balance, electrolytes as needed

### Week 2-4: Active Recovery and Base Rebuilding
- **Maintain balanced nutrition**
- **Avoid caloric deficit** (preserve muscle mass and energy)
- **Ensure adequate protein** for ongoing repair
- **Monitor body weight** (avoid unintended loss)

### Dietary Requirements (always applicable)
- **Gluten-free** (required)
- **Dairy-free** (required)

## Strength and Mobility Integration

### Strength Training Phases

**Phase 1 (Days 1-7):** Complete rest or very light bodyweight mobility

**Phase 2 (Days 8-14):** Light strength, 2x/week
- Bodyweight squats, lunges, planks, glute bridges
- Focus: Movement quality, no fatigue

**Phase 3 (Days 15-28):** Normal runner strength, 2x/week
- Tue: Primary session (moderate-heavy resistance)
- Fri: Lighter session (activation, stability)
- Exercises: Deadlifts, single-leg work, core, calf raises

**Phase 4 (Days 29+):** Full strength program
- Integrated with running training
- Coordinated to avoid interference with quality runs

### Mobility Work Phases

**Phase 1 (Days 1-7):** Daily gentle stretching and foam rolling
- Focus: Calves, quads, hips, IT band, feet
- Duration: 10-15 min/day

**Phase 2 (Days 8-14):** Continue daily + longer Sunday session
- Daily: 10-15 min
- Sunday: 30-45 min

**Phase 3 (Days 15-28):** Return to normal schedule
- Daily brief work + Sunday longer session
- Address any lingering tightness

**Phase 4 (Days 29+):** Full mobility program
- Supports next training cycle
- Integrated with strength and running

---

## Mental and Psychological Recovery

Marathon training and racing are mentally taxing. Recovery includes psychological restoration:

### Immediate Post-Marathon (Week 1-2)
- **Allow mental break from structure**
- **Enjoy rest without guilt**
- **Reflect on race experience** (what worked, what didn't)
- **Celebrate the achievement**

### Weeks 3-4
- **Gradual return to training mindset**
- **Set new goals** (e.g., Gasparilla Half Marathon)
- **Begin planning next cycle with renewed motivation**

### Weeks 5-6
- **Mental readiness for structured training**
- **Excitement about new challenge**
- **Lessons from marathon inform next training block**

**Important:** If motivation is low or burnout is present, extend recovery. Long-term consistency requires mental freshness.

## Coordination with Other Coaches

### Strength Coach
- Coordinate strength resumption with running progression
- Avoid heavy lower-body work within 24 hours of runs during Phases 2-3
- Full strength program resumes in Phase 4

### Mobility Coach
- Daily light work throughout recovery
- Longer Sunday sessions resume in Phase 2
- Full program in Phase 4

### Nutrition Coach
- Support recovery nutrition (protein, anti-inflammatory foods)
- Monitor body weight and energy balance
- Ensure adequate fueling for base rebuilding in Phase 3

## Reassessing VDOT for Next Training Cycle

Before beginning the next structured training cycle (Gasparilla Half Marathon prep), reassess current VDOT to set accurate training paces.

### When to Reassess
**Recommended timing:** 5-6 weeks post-marathon (Days 36-42 of recovery)

### Why Reassess
- Marathon training and taper may have changed fitness
- Recovery period may have slightly reduced VO2max
- Post-race fatigue needs to clear before accurate assessment
- Fresh workout data provides better baseline than race performance

### How to Reassess (choose one)

**Option 1: Threshold Time Trial**
- Warmup: 15 min E + 4 strides
- Main: 2 × 1 mile at T effort (comfortably hard, not all-out) with 3 min jog
- Cooldown: 10 min E
- Use average mile pace to calculate VDOT

**Option 2: 5K Time Trial**
- Warmup: 15 min E + 6 strides
- Main: 5K at hard, controlled effort (race simulation)
- Cooldown: 10 min E
- Use 5K time for VDOT calculation

**Option 3: Half Marathon Race (Gasparilla)**
- Use actual race performance to calculate VDOT
- Apply to training cycles after Gasparilla

### Applying New VDOT
Once VDOT is reassessed:
- Update data/athlete/current_training_status.md
- Recalculate E, M, T, I, R paces using Jack Daniels VDOT tables
- Use new paces for all structured workouts in next training cycle

- Reduce volume by 40-50%

## Final Notes

This framework is a guideline, not a rigid protocol. Individual recovery varies based on:
- Marathon effort and performance
- Pre-race training load and fatigue
- Age and training history
- Sleep quality and life stress
- Environmental conditions

**Key Principle:** It is better to extend recovery by a few days than to rush back and risk injury or prolonged fatigue.

Trust the process. Recovery is not lost fitness. It is the foundation for your next training cycle.
