# Athlete Goals – Neil Stagner

This document describes the athlete’s goals across performance, training, health, and long-term development.  
Agents should use these goals to guide recommendations, maintain alignment across disciplines, and ensure training decisions support long-term progress.

---

## 1. Primary Performance Goals

### A. Marathon Performance
- Achieve a **4:00 marathon** (approx. **9:10/mi** pace) at a major race.
- Execute an evenly paced race supported by strong fueling and hydration.
- Arrive at race day feeling fresh, not overtrained.

### B. Half Marathon Performance
- Continue improving half marathon times, targeting **sub-1:50** as a medium-term milestone.
- Maintain the ability to perform well in warm/humid conditions.

---

## 2. Short-Term Training Goals (Current Training Phase)

- Maintain consistency with a **4-day running schedule**.
- Complete scheduled weekly quality sessions and long runs when recovery allows.
- Improve ability to sustain **marathon-pace (M)** efforts for extended durations.
- Maintain resilience against fatigue during threshold (T) and long-run efforts.
- Continue refining race-day fueling strategy.

---

## 3. Long-Term Development Goals

- Build durable aerobic capacity capable of supporting annual marathon cycles.
- Improve running economy through:
  - consistent aerobic development  
  - quality strength training  
  - mobility and biomechanical efficiency  
- Increase ability to adapt training in response to:
  - fatigue  
  - work schedule  
  - variability in sleep and life responsibilities  

- Establish a sustainable training foundation that:
  - supports running performance  
  - avoids chronic injury  
  - remains compatible with work and family life  

---

## 4. Strength & Mobility Goals

### Strength Goals
- Develop strong, balanced lower-body musculature:
  - glutes, hamstrings, quads, calves  
- Build core stability to support efficient running form.
- Increase fatigue resistance during long runs and marathon-pace segments.
- Avoid strength sessions that cause excessive DOMS or interfere with key runs.

### Mobility Goals
- Maintain healthy mobility in:
  - ankles and feet  
  - calves  
  - hips  
  - hamstrings  
  - thoracic spine  

- Prevent flare-ups of **Posterior Tibialis Tendinopathy** through regular targeted mobility and strengthening.

---

## 5. Health & Lifestyle Goals

- Support recovery with adequate sleep when possible.
- Maintain a healthy body weight and energy balance.
- Prevent excessive fatigue or overreaching.
- Keep stress manageable during demanding periods.
- Fuel effectively for training and recovery.

---

## 6. Consistency & Training Lifestyle Goals

- Build a long-term habit of sustainable, flexible training that integrates with work, family, and life responsibilities.
- Maintain enjoyment of the training process.
- Develop training resilience that adapts to variable schedules and recovery capacity.

---

## 7. Race Execution Goals

- Arrive at key races:
  - adequately tapered  
  - healthy  
  - confident in pacing  
  - confident in fueling strategy  

- Execute race strategy with:
  - even pacing  
  - appropriate early-race restraint  
  - well-practiced fueling and hydration  

- Minimize GI issues, dehydration, and performance drop-off in later miles.

---

This document should be referenced by all agents to ensure training, strength, mobility, and nutrition decisions support the athlete’s short- and long-term goals.
