# Training Preferences – Neil Stagner

This document describes personal training preferences that should guide how agents deliver recommendations.  
These are preferences, not hard constraints, and should be applied alongside the actual training plan,
profile, and training history.

---

## 1. Coaching Style Preferences

- Prefers **direct, clear, and structured guidance** instead of vague descriptions.
- Likes workouts broken down into **step-by-step segments**.
- Appreciates **explanations of the purpose** behind each workout or adjustment.
- Favors **options** when fatigue or scheduling issues arise:
  - Conservative option  
  - Moderate option  
  - Full-intent version  

---

## 2. Scheduling & Work-Life Integration Preferences

### Work Schedule
- Works **four days per week**: **Monday–Thursday**, **0700–1730** in-office.
- Commute, family responsibilities, and work hours limit training flexibility on these days.

### Fitness Hours Availability
- Has **3 hours per week** of dedicated fitness time granted by work.
- Typically used for **morning training sessions around 0715**.
- These hours are ideal for:
  - Runs under 60 minutes
  - Thursday morning quality sessions  
  - Optional easy run mornings if recovery is good  

### Scheduling Preferences
- Prefers **early morning workouts** on workdays.
- Avoids evening sessions due to:
  - family obligations  
  - newborn care  
  - fatigue from long workdays  

### Weekend Flexibility
- Friday, Saturday, and Sunday provide more flexibility.
- Long runs are preferred on Saturdays.
- Sunday is ideal for a longer mobility/yoga block.

### Agent Guidance Expectations
- Favor **morning workouts** for workdays.
- Avoid long/intense weekday evenings.
- Keep weekday sessions **time-efficient**.
- Schedule demanding or long sessions on weekends.

---

## 3. Training Structure Preferences

- Prefers a **consistent weekly cadence**, especially:
  - Mon: Easy  
  - Tue: Strength  
  - Wed: Easy  
  - Thu: Quality  
  - Fri: Light strength  
  - Sat: Long run  
  - Sun: Mobility/Yoga  

- Strong preference for **time-based workouts** instead of distance.
- Likes **Jack Daniels–style zone labeling** (E, M, T, I, R).
- Wants **periodized training**:
  - Base → Early Quality → Race-Specific → Taper.

### Flexibility Preference
- While the above structure is preferred, **the athlete is flexible**.
- The coach should:
  - Allow deviations from the weekly structure when needed.  
  - Provide **alternative scheduling options** if life, work, newborn care, or sleep interfere.
  - Encourage adjustments that preserve training intent even if the day shifts.
  - Avoid rigidity — emphasize **principles over exact day-by-day adherence**.

---

## 4. Adjustment & Recovery Preferences

- Prefers conservative adjustments when:
  - sleep is poor  
  - recovery metrics are low  
  - fatigue accumulates  
  - soreness or early injury signals appear  

- Values **adaptive coaching**, especially with:
  - newborn-related sleep interruptions  
  - variable daily energy  

- When compromised, prefers:
  - Reducing intensity first  
  - Preserving easy-day consistency  
  - Avoiding back-to-back hard sessions  

---

## 5. Strength Training Preferences

- Strength should **support running**, not compete with it.
- Prefers:
  - Core, glutes, calves, hamstrings  
  - Stability and single-leg work  
  - Low soreness before key runs  

- Does **not** prefer:
  - High-DOMS sessions close to quality runs  
  - Sudden introduction of unfamiliar movements  

- Ideal structure:
  - **Tuesday** = primary/heavier session  
  - **Friday** = lighter session  

---

## 6. Mobility & Recovery Preferences

- Prefers **short, actionable routines** (5–15 min) during the week.
- Sunday is preferred for a **longer mobility/yoga session**.
- Likes routines targeting:
  - hips  
  - hamstrings  
  - calves  
  - ankles/feet  
  - thoracic spine  

- Prefers mobility that:
  - supports upcoming training  
  - reduces stiffness from sitting at work  
  - avoids causing residual soreness  

---

## 7. Communication Preferences

- Prefers **simple, direct pacing or effort ranges** when needed.
- Likes:
  - RPE cues  
  - Jack Daniels zone labels  
  - Guidance on when to slow down  
  - Heat/humidity adjustment cues  

- Appreciates “If/Then” guidance:
  - If HR is unusually high → slow down  
  - If sleep < 5 hrs → adjust intensity  
  - If soreness persists → reduce load  

---

## 8. Race & Workout Simulation Preferences

- Prefers to:
  - Use race shoes during key long-run workouts
  - Practice fueling during long runs
  - Simulate race pacing and timing
  - Avoid new gear/nutrition close to race day

### Dietary Requirements
- **Gluten-free** (required)
- **Dairy-free** (required)
- All nutrition recommendations must respect these constraints

### Preferred Fueling Products
Based on successful historical usage:
- **Gels:** Precision Fuel, Huma gels (gluten-free, dairy-free options)
- **Electrolytes:** Base salts, FastChews
- **Hydration:** Water with electrolyte supplementation
- Open to testing alternatives during training (not near race day)

---

## 9. Data Interpretation Preferences

- Uses Garmin metrics as helpful signals, not absolute truths.
- Prefers:
  - Trend-based interpretation  
  - RPE-first intensity guidance  

- Values context-aware analysis:
  - heat/dew point  
  - cumulative workload  
  - sleep debt  

---

## 10. Safety & Long-Term Preferences

- Strong preference for:
  - avoiding injury  
  - sustainable progression  
  - respecting warning signs  

- Does not want:
  - Forcing intensity on low-energy days  
  - Making up missed workouts with harder efforts  
  - Unnecessary stacking of demanding sessions
